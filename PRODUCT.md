# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The primary audience is the OpenAI Buildathon jury and demo attendees. They need to
understand Pulso's value in a few seconds, remember its four differentiators, and have a
fast path to open the live demo from the presentation screen.

## Product Purpose

Pulso turns citizen observations about urban incidents into a verified, real-time information
network. The pitch surface must make the product feel useful, trustworthy, and immediately
demonstrable rather than presenting it as another incident map.

## Brand Personality

Pulso is **confiable, humano y resolutivo**. It should create confidence that the information
has a real person behind it, empathy for people navigating a city, and a sense that the product
helps someone decide what to do next.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards with repeated feature cards and invented growth metrics.
- Alarmist emergency posters that create fear without showing a concrete action.
- Dense technical architecture diagrams as the primary pitch message.
- Visual decoration that competes with the map, the headline, or the QR call to action.

## Design Principles

- Show the decision Pulso enables, not only the technology behind it.
- Make trust visible through verified identity and grounded data language.
- Use one strong visual scene, the living city map, instead of a collection of unrelated UI
  panels.
- Keep the message readable from a projector and the demo path one scan away.
- Never claim a performance metric until the demo run has measured it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

The pitch slide must use high-contrast text, readable type at presentation distance, meaningful
alt text for the QR, and a `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback. Color must not be the only way to
distinguish incident types; labels and icons should reinforce the map markers.
